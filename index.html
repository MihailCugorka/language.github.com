! The Last of the Phoenicians

Many writing systems that we use today descended directly from the right-to-left "abjad" (no vowels) created by Phoenicians in 1050 BC. Oddlt, the first "letter" of the alphabet represent a glottal stop, as in dene.

  • alf ("ox") ʾ [ʔ] u10900
  • bet ("house") b [b] u10901
  • gaml ("camel") g [ɡ] u10902
  • ...

it's is all ελληνικά to me

Many writing systems that we use today descended directly from the alphabet created by Greeks in 800 BC. They used the letters of their alphabet to represent numbers as well.

  • Α α 1 alpha [a] [aː], [a]
  • Β β 2 beta [b], [v] => b, v
  • Γ γ 3 gamma [ɡ], [ɣ] => g, gh, y
  • ...

Three extra characters were added the phonetic symbols to complete the set necessary in their chosen writing system.

  • ϛ 6 digamma
  • ϟ 90 koppa
  • ϡ 900 sampi
